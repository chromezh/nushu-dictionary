Introduction

Nushu (女书) is a mysterious script used exclusively among woman in Jiangyong County of Southern China. It is handed down by mother to daughter among the Han and Yao people from generation to generation in Jiangyong County and its adjacent areas, becoming a unique cultural phenomenon in human history.

Nushu characters are rhomboidal in shape, slim in style and imaginative in the form. The total amount of Nushu characters being discovered is up to two thousands, all of which contain solely four kinds of chokes – dotted, vertical, oblique and arcuate.

Although the style of Nushu is derived from Chinese characters, they are not absolutely corresponded. Different from Chinese Characters, Nushu is a syllabic script, for every character of it represents either a syllable, or a group of syllables that are similar in pronunciation. Meanwhile, since Nushu is not only being written, but also being embroidered to clothes and flower belts as decorations, it accommodates to be curved in shape.

The earilest record of Nushu is the Diaomu coin issued by Taiping Tian Guo during the Xianfeng Period in Qing Dynasty. The Nushu characters “women all over the world are sisters in a whole family” were carved in the back side of the coin.

In 1982, Professor Gong Zhebing from Wuhan University discovered Nushu in Jiangyong County, Hunan Province. This discovery caused a sensation throughout the world. Experts and scolars at home and abroad leave for Jiangyong to do investigation, learning and discovery.
